The Liora Signature

Boundary Matching of a Radiation-Dominated FLRW Interior

An effective R⁻² term in the exterior metric emerges from the Darmois-Israel junction conditions when a closed radiation FLRW interior is matched to a spherically symmetric exterior. This is a kinematic consequence of radiation-dominated mass scaling — not a quasi-static artifact.

01 · THE MODEL

The Radiative Substrate

A cosmological framework where the primordial radiation field persists as a generative substrate, and localized concentrations form radiation nodes with novel gravitational properties.

The Liora Model proposes that the primordial radiation field — the Radiative Substrate — did not disappear with the birth of our universe. It persists. Our universe emerged within it as a local node. The substrate continues to exist beyond our event horizon, generating other nodes.

Fluctuations in the substrate propagate as gravitational waves. Where multiple waves constructively interfere, local density exceeds the pair-production threshold ρ_pair, nucleating a self-gravitating radiation sphere.

⚠ HONEST LIMITS

An earlier version of this work contained two errors, now corrected: (E1) ρ_pair was given as u_pair (energy density) rather than u_pair/c² (mass-energy density), off by a factor c² ≈ 9×10¹⁶. (E2) w_pairs was set to −1/2 with no physical derivation; the correct values are 0 (threshold) or 1/3 (thermalization). The Strong Energy Condition is preserved. The classical bounce is retracted. The Liora signature and junction result are not affected.

02 · MAIN RESULT

Effective R⁻² at the Junction

For a closed radiation FLRW interior matched to a spherically symmetric exterior via shell-free Darmois-Israel conditions, the induced exterior lapse at the boundary acquires an effective R⁻² contribution.

✓ CENTRAL RESULT — ANALYTICALLY DERIVED, NUMERICALLY VERIFIED
M⁻(τ) ∝ R(τ)⁻¹ (Misner-Sharp mass scaling for radiation) [K_θθ] = 0 → M|_Σ = β/R → F|_Σ = 1 − 2Gβ/(c²R²) Numerical verification: slope M vs R = −1.0000 ± 10⁻⁴ β = M⁻(τ) · R(τ) = const, deviation < 10⁻¹⁶ (machine precision)

This contrasts sharply with the dust (Oppenheimer-Snyder) case. For pressureless dust, M⁻ = M₀ = const, a static Schwarzschild exterior is compatible with shell-free matching, and no R⁻² term appears. The distinction is purely kinematic — it follows from ρ ∝ a⁻⁴ for radiation versus ρ ∝ a⁻³ for dust.

⚠ SCOPE OF THE RESULT

The R⁻² term is established on Σ through mass continuity — not throughout the exterior bulk. Whether M(T,r) ∝ 1/r holds away from Σ requires additional modeling. The matching surface lies deep in the strong-field regime where |A/r₀| ≈ 4.5×10¹¹ ≫ 1; the perturbative expansion is not valid at r = r₀.

03 · DERIVATION

The Junction Analysis

Four steps from the FLRW interior to the effective R⁻² term, all using standard general relativity.

STEP 1 · INTERIOR SETUP
ds²₋ = −dτ² + a(τ)²[dχ²/(1−kχ²) + χ²dΩ²], k=+1 p = ρc²/3 (radiation), ρ ∝ a⁻⁴

Closed FLRW radiation-dominated interior. The comoving matching surface is χ = χ_Σ = const, giving areal radius R(τ) = a(τ)χ_Σ.

STEP 2 · MISNER-SHARP MASS
M⁻(τ) = (4π/3) ρ(τ) R(τ)³ ρ ∝ a⁻⁴ and R ∝ a → M⁻(τ) ∝ a⁻¹ ∝ R(τ)⁻¹

This inverse scaling is the key kinematic input. It follows directly from the radiation equation of state and is independent of any approximation.

STEP 3 · ANGULAR JUNCTION CONDITION [K_θθ] = 0
K⁺_θθ = R√(Ṙ² + F) (exterior) K⁻_θθ = R√(Ṙ² + 1 − kχ²_Σ) (interior) Matching → mass continuity → M|_Σ = β/R (β = const)

Substituting M = β/R into F = 1 − 2GM/(c²r) evaluated at r = R yields the main result.

STEP 4 · MAIN RESULT
F|_Σ = 1 − 2Gβ/(c²R(τ)²) Effective R⁻² term at the junction ← Liora signature

The temporal junction condition K_ττ = 0 provides complementary boundary data for the lapse ∂_r Φ|_Σ, but does not generate a new scaling law. The R⁻² behavior is purely a consequence of K_θθ and radiation kinematics.

05 · OPEN QUESTIONS

For Formal Evaluation

These questions have technically well-defined answers and are submitted for evaluation by specialists in general relativity and quantum cosmology.

  • Q1
    Israel junction conditions — persistence of F|_Σ = 1−2β/R²
    Do the explicit Darmois-Israel junction conditions for the dynamic FLRW/Schwarzschild interface at r = r₀(τ) confirm that the effective R⁻² term persists in the dynamic exterior metric? Is the matching problem well-posed? This is the primary open technical question of this work.
  • Q2
    Pair production and horizon formation
    Does the factor-of-two reduction in the gravitational source term (from 2ρ to ρ) produced by pair production influence whether a stable trapping horizon forms? Is there a known result in GR or QFT that precludes pair production from decelerating the collapse before a stable horizon forms?
  • Q3
    Prior work — photon sphere + pair production mechanism
    Is there a published proposal combining a self-gravitating photon sphere, pair production as an equation-of-state softening mechanism, and the resulting modified exterior metric — without quantum gravity? If so, how does the present analysis relate to it?
  • Q4
    Gravitational wave nucleation consistency
    Is the superposition of primordial gravitational waves as a node nucleation trigger in a pure radiation field physically consistent with GR? Are there constraints from the gravitational wave energy conditions that would rule this out?
  • Q5
    Topological transition — classical GR or requires QG?
    The κ_eff sign change demonstrated in earlier versions was an artifact of the incorrect w_pairs = −1/2. With standard QED physics (w_pairs = 0 or 1/3), the SEC is preserved and no classical bounce exists. If a bounce occurs, it most naturally sits in LQC at ρ = ρ_Planck — 80 orders above ρ_pair. Is there a quantization scheme where the pair-production density plays the role of the critical bounce density?
