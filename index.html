The Liora Model Signature

Boundary Matching of a Radiation-Dominated FLRW Interior

An effective R⁻² term in the exterior metric emerges from the Darmois-Israel junction conditions when a closed radiation FLRW interior is matched to a spherically symmetric exterior. This is a kinematic consequence of radiation-dominated mass scaling.

01 · THE MODEL

The Radiative Substrate

A cosmological framework where the primordial radiation field persists as a generative substrate, and localized concentrations form radiation nodes with novel gravitational properties.

The Liora Model proposes that the primordial radiation field — the Radiative Substrate — did not disappear with the birth of our universe. It persists. Our universe emerged within it as a local node. The substrate continues to exist beyond our event horizon, generating other nodes.

01 · PIECE
Modified exterior metric
The exterior metric of the radiation node contains a B/r² correction term absent from Schwarzschild — the Liora signature — arising from the extended mass distribution.
02 · PIECE
Pair production dynamics
Photon-photon pair production occurs 37 orders of magnitude faster than gravitational collapse. Under standard QED, the Strong Energy Condition is preserved.
03 · PIECE
Junction condition analysis
The Darmois-Israel conditions at the comoving boundary Σ force M⁻(τ) ∝ R(τ)⁻¹, which induces an effective R⁻² term in the exterior lapse.
