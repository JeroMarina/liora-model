01 · THE PHYSICS

Analytic Derivation

The Liora Model examines the junction between a closed, radiation-filled interior and a vacuum exterior. Unlike the standard dust model, radiation density scales as $a^{-4}$, which fundamentally alters the mass-radius relationship at the boundary.

03 · PEER REVIEW

Discussion Points

Are the Israel Junction Conditions satisfied?
Yes. The continuity of the induced metric $[h_{ab}]=0$ and the extrinsic curvature $[K_{ab}]=0$ (for a shell-less boundary) were used to derive the scaling.
How does this differ from Schwarzschild?
A standard Schwarzschild exterior assumes a constant mass $M$. Our model allows $M$ to be a function of the junction radius $R(\tau)$, introducing the $1/r^2$ term in the effective lapse.
