The Liora Signature

Boundary Matching of a Radiation-Dominated FLRW Interior

An effective $R^{-2}$ term in the exterior metric emerges from the Darmois-Israel junction conditions when a closed radiation FLRW interior is matched to a spherically symmetric exterior.

01 · THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK

The Radiative Substrate

The Liora Model proposes that the primordial radiation field persists as a generative substrate. Unlike the standard dust-dominated collapse models (Oppenheimer-Snyder), radiation density scales as $\rho \propto a^{-4}$. This fundamental kinematic difference forces a non-constant mass-radius relationship at the junction.

Metric Matching

Using the Darmois-Israel junction conditions, we prove that the continuity of the extrinsic curvature requires a specific mass scaling at the boundary.

Signature Emergence

This scaling manifests in the exterior as a gravitational potential correction term that drops off as $1/r^2$.

02 · CENTRAL DERIVATION

Analytic Solution

1. Interior Metric: ds²₋ = −dτ² + a(τ)²[dχ²/(1−kχ²) + χ²dΩ²] 2. Mass Scaling on Σ: M⁻(τ) = (4π/3) ρ(τ) R(τ)³ ∝ R(τ)⁻¹ 3. Exterior Lapse Result: F(r) = 1 − 2Gβ / (c²r²)

The transition from a volume-proportional mass (dust) to an inverse-radius mass (radiation) is the defining mechanism of the Liora Model.

04 · OPEN QUESTIONS

Critical Analysis

Q: Does the $R^{-2}$ term violate the Strong Energy Condition?
No. Since the interior is a standard radiation-dominated FLRW solution, the Strong Energy Condition ($\rho + 3p \geq 0$) is maintained throughout the interior and at the junction boundary.
Q: What happens at the $R \to 0$ limit?
Like all FLRW models, the solution approaches a singularity. However, the Liora Model suggests that pair-production dynamics may provide a pressure-driven cutoff before the singularity is reached.
